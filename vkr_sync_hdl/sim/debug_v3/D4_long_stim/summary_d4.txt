Observations
------------
1. MER is essentially flat across trim depths (12.40 → 12.53 dB).
   The transient contributes at most 0.13 dB to the metric measured
   on the standard N_SYM=2000 stimulus; the apparent 12.7 dB value
   is the steady-state MER, NOT a transient inflation.

2. Internal-state metrics (μ_lock mean/std, debug_w, vi_reg) are
   trim-independent — the steady_start window in analyse_v3.py
   already excludes any transient.

3. The longer run reveals modest seed-42 noise: vi_reg drifts from
   −14 (N=2000) to −18 (N=8000), consistent with the negative
   integrator bias accumulating slowly under random e. μ_lock ratio
   stays at 0.76, MER stays at 12.4–12.5 dB. No instability or
   cycle-slips over the full 7980 symbols.

Conclusion
----------
The 12.7 dB MER plateau at the working point is a true steady-state
property; lengthening the stimulus does NOT reveal additional gain
or convergence to a higher MER. This rules out "still in transient"
as a possible explanation for the gap to ТЗ target.
